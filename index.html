Why "membrane"?

Everything alive has an edge.

A cell holds its shape with a membrane — a living boundary that flexes and stretches, but only so far. Your body works the same way. There's a limit to how much you can take and still bounce back.

Stay inside it and you adapt, recover, and grow. Push past it and you break down. Membrane Health finds your edge from your own data and shows you where you stand inside it — every single day. That space between where you are and where your edge is? That's how much you've got left.

Timing is everything

It's not just what your body is doing — it's when.

Timing reaches into far more than clocks or schedules. It shapes whether the movements of life arrive in an order the body can actually receive, organize, and use well.

Your body isn't a set of dials with good and bad levels — it's a web of rhythms that have to stay in time with one another, and when that timing slips you often feel it before any single number looks wrong. So Membrane Health reads your timing, not just your state: the order things arrive in, the window in the day when effort will land, how many days until you're recovered, and where you are in your own cycle. Timing isn't a feature here — it's the axis everything is built around.

The road ahead

Reading your body's coherence — with light.

The daily read you get today sits on top of a deeper research program. Its question is the same one the whole app is built around: not how high your numbers are, but how well your body keeps time with itself.

We're building a research-grade instrument that measures exactly that — a controlled pulse of light from your phone, and a map of how distributed parts of the body respond together, in phase. It's called phase-coherence imaging. It's patent-pending, and like everything here, it's measurement, not diagnosis.

A preview of work in development — not yet in the app. When it's ready, your phone will see something no wearable can.
